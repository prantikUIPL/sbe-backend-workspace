## 1. What the spec asks (item #7, verbatim intent)

> **Send Order Email** — On the Order Details page, the admin selects an email template from a dropdown, then either saves the order or sends the email.
> Dropdown options: **New Order Confirm/Invoice · Cancelled Order · Invoice/Payment Reminder with link.**

This is a **manual, admin-triggered** email send. The complication is that two of the three options overlap with **already-delivered automated email flows**, and the third ("with link") depends on infrastructure that doesn't exist yet. Those overlaps and gaps are what need client direction before build.

---

## 2. Current build state (why these questions exist)

Investigated across all five repos + the delivered story docs. Summary:

- **No manual "send order email with a chosen template" endpoint exists.** The only on-demand order emails today are two fixed actions: `POST /orders/:id/resend-confirmation` (always the booth confirmation, invoice **PDF attached**, no template choice, throttled 5 min) and `POST /orders/:id/resend-portal-password`. There is no template dropdown, no order-scoped template-list endpoint, and no `orders.send_email` permission.
- **"Cancelled Order"** email (`order_canceled`) exists but fires **automatically as a side-effect of the cancel/refund action** (Story 24.11, SBE-1135) — there is no standalone "just send the cancellation email" path. This flow has **no dedupe** — a manual re-send would silently deliver a duplicate.
- **"Invoice/Payment Reminder with link"** is the largest gap: the only reminder templates (`payment_reminder_upcoming` / `payment_reminder_overdue`, Story 24.15, SBE-1139) are **cron-only**, fire **only for split-payment, non-credit-card installments**, and contain **no link or PDF at all**. They do have a coarse email+slug+time-window dedupe, which a manual send could interfere with (one suppressing the other).
- **There is no signed/emailable invoice link anywhere.** The only invoice URL is a raw, **unsigned CloudFront URL** with a **guessable** key (`exports/invoices/{company_id}/{invoice_number}.pdf`); the code is internally contradictory about whether that URL is even publicly openable. Exhibitor download is auth-gated but ultimately returns the same raw URL. Current admin emails sidestep this by **attaching the PDF bytes** rather than linking.

**Net:** item #7 is effectively unbuilt, and building it naïvely would duplicate/conflict with 24.11 and 24.15 and lean on a non-existent secure invoice link. Hence the questions below.

## 3. Open questions

Each question notes **why it's open** (the gap) and **our recommendation** (for discussion, not a decision).

### A. Template set & purpose

**Q1 — Is the dropdown a fixed three-option list, or driven dynamically by the notification-template registry?**
*Why:* The Email & SMS system already supports admin-created custom templates that can share a trigger with a predefined one. A registry-driven dropdown would surface those; a fixed list would not.
*Recommendation:* Fixed, curated list of order-relevant templates for v1 (predictable for QA and permissions); revisit dynamic sourcing later.

**Q2 — Does "New Order Confirm/Invoice" cover all order types, or booth orders only?**
*Why:* Today booth and PPL confirmations are **separate** templates (`booth_order_confirmation` vs `ppl_order_confirmation`); the existing resend only handles the booth one.
*Recommendation:* Confirm scope = product/booth orders (matching the Order Management surface); PPL/subscription confirmations remain the PPL module's surface.

**Q3 — Should the admin preview/edit the email before sending, or is it a fixed-template blind send?**
*Why:* Determines whether we need a preview endpoint and whether per-send edits are persisted.
*Recommendation:* Preview (read-only) yes; per-send body editing no (templates are centrally managed in the Email & SMS epic).

### B. "Cancelled Order" option vs the delivered auto-email (24.11)

**Q4 — What is the intended purpose of a *manual* "Cancelled Order" send, given 24.11 already emails the customer automatically on cancel/refund?**
*Why:* If it's a re-send (customer lost it, or notification was unchecked at cancel time), fine. If it's meant to notify **without** cancelling, that's a different behavior.
*Recommendation:* Treat it strictly as a **re-send of the last cancellation/refund email**, selectable **only for orders that are already cancelled/refunded**. Never a way to send a "cancelled" notice for an active order.

**Q5 — Is duplicate delivery acceptable, or do we need idempotency + a "last sent" indicator?**
*Why:* 24.11 has **zero dedupe**; a manual send will duplicate with nothing detecting the prior automated send.
*Recommendation:* Add a "last sent on … by …" indicator on Order Details and a light throttle (like the existing 5-min resend guard); accept that an intentional re-send delivers again.

### C. "Invoice/Payment Reminder with link" option vs the delivered cron (24.15)

**Q6 — What is the scope of the manual reminder?**
*Why:* The automated reminder covers **only split-payment, non-credit-card installments**. The manual option's scope is unspecified — any order with a balance? credit-card installments? one-time (non-plan) invoices?
*Recommendation:* Client to define. Simplest: any product order with an outstanding balance, regardless of payment method.

**Q7 — Should the manual send and the automated cron interfere?**
*Why:* They'd share slugs and the cron's coarse email/time-window dedupe, so a manual send could **suppress the next automated reminder** (and vice-versa).
*Recommendation:* Give the manual send a **distinct slug** (or bypass the cron dedupe) so manual and automated reminders don't silence each other.

**Q8 — What exactly is "the link"?**
*Why:* No pay-now link exists anywhere today; the reminder templates carry no link at all.
*Recommendation:* Client to specify: a **pay-now/payment** link vs an **invoice-PDF** link. This drives Q9.

### D. The invoice link itself (blocks Q2, Q6, Q8)

**Q9 — How should an emailed invoice be delivered, given no secure emailable link exists today?**
*Why:* The only invoice URL is unsigned, non-expiring, and has a **guessable** key. Options:
- **(a) Public link** — reuse the existing CloudFront URL. *Risk:* guessable, forwardable, never expires → anyone can view the invoice, no login.
- **(b) Signed, expiring link** — secure, but **needs to be built** (URL signing isn't applied to invoices anywhere).
- **(c) Attach the PDF** — what the current confirmation email does; safe, but **contradicts the "with link" wording**.
*Recommendation:* **(b)** for anything sent to customers; **(c)** as an interim if (b) can't make the release. Avoid (a).

**Q10 — If a public link is chosen, is unauthenticated, guessable access to invoices acceptable to the client?**
*Why:* Explicit sign-off needed given the exposure (invoices contain billing details).
*Recommendation:* Not acceptable without at least signed URLs; flag as a security decision.

### E. Recipients & send mechanics

**Q11 — Who receives a manually sent email?**
*Why:* Delivered flows differ — cancel uses `billing_email` → primary-exhibitor fallback; the reminder uses `billing_email` only. 24.6 also lets admins edit an additional-emails CC list.
*Recommendation:* `billing_email` + the order's additional-emails CC list, with the primary-exhibitor fallback when billing email is missing; optionally show the resolved recipients before send.

**Q12 — "Save order OR send email" — two separate actions, or does sending also persist the order?**
*Why:* The spec phrasing is ambiguous and affects the UI/endpoint contract.
*Recommendation:* Two distinct actions — "Save" persists billing edits; "Send email" dispatches without requiring a save. Client to confirm.

**Q13 — Should manual sends be audited and throttled?**
*Why:* The existing resend is throttled and logs; consistency + support-traceability.
*Recommendation:* Yes — audit each send (who/what/when/to whom) on Order Details, and throttle to prevent accidental spamming.

**Q14 — Permission model: one `orders.send_email` permission, or per-template?**
*Why:* No send permission exists yet; sending a cancellation vs a reminder may warrant different gates.
*Recommendation:* Start with a single `orders.send_email`; split per-template only if the client wants differentiated control.

### F. Channel

**Q15 — Confirm email-only (no SMS) for this feature.**
*Why:* The system is email-only today (no SMS provider integrated), and the spec item is titled "Send Order **Email**."
*Recommendation:* Confirm email-only; SMS is out of scope until the SMS-provider story lands.
