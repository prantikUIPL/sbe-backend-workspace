## Summary

Story 24.2 adds three backend-enforced, combinable filters (date range, Show City, Sales Channel) to the net-new Order Management list, plus an empty state and date validation (End not before Start). There is no order-management module yet, but admin-backend-api already ships a near-identical pattern in the admin/ppl-order list (Order-table query with created_at gte/lte date-range filtering, from<=to validation, pagination, search, status filter, empty result) — so date-range, validation, combinability, empty-state and backend-enforcement are firmly Deliverable by reusing that pattern. The Show City filter (24.2-c) is Deliverable with exclude-unmatched semantics: derived via OrderItem.show_product_id -> ShowProduct.show_id -> Shows.city_id -> City, which is written on cart-originated product orders (admin checkout.service + exhibitor cart.helpers via materializeCartToOrder); the OM list is scoped to order_type = product, so subscription/ppl_addon orders are out of scope, and product orders with no show linkage correctly never match a Show City filter (documented caveat, not a coverage bug). The Show City dropdown (24.2-d) is Delegated to the already-built `GET /api/v1/cities` (city-management module) — no new endpoint and no work in this story. The Sales Channel filter (24.2-e) is Deliverable: the product-only scope guarantees every in-scope order carries a cart_id (only subscription/ppl_addon have null cart_id, and those are out of scope), so cart.created_by_type is always present and gives a clean, total 2-way mapping (admin = Admin Role/Sales Order, exhibitor = Self-Service Portal Order) with no coverage gap. Overall feasible now; the only remaining documented caveat is the show-city one (non-show-linked product orders never match a Show City filter).

## Open Questions

- **[RESOLVED 2026-07-02]** Sales Channel: the spec defines exactly two values (Admin Role/Sales Order, Self-Service Portal Order). There is no order-level channel column; the distinction is derived from cart.created_by_type. The prior concern was that orders with null cart_id carry no discriminator.
  - **Decision (2026-07-02):** The OM list is scoped to order_type = product, and product orders always carry cart_id, so no null-cart orders exist in scope — the exclude-vs-default question is moot. 24.2-e is Deliverable: the filter maps cart.created_by_type admin -> Admin Role/Sales Order, exhibitor -> Self-Service Portal Order, with no coverage gap.
- **[RESOLVED 2026-07-02]** Show City: orders with no show linkage (subscription/ppl_addon orders, and product orders created by exhibitor payments.service which omit show_product_id) cannot be matched to a show city. When a Show City filter is applied, should these orders be excluded entirely (no city match)?
  - **Decision (2026-07-02):** 24.2-c is Deliverable with exclude-unmatched semantics — build the nested orderItems->showProduct->show.city_id relation filter; product orders with no show linkage correctly don't match. Documented caveat: those non-show-linked product orders never appear under a Show City filter.
